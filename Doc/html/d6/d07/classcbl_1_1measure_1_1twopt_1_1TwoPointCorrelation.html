the Poisson errors

This function computes the Poisson errors associated to the natural or Landy&Szalay estimators of the two-point correlation function.

The formula implemented is the following:

\[ \delta\xi = \sqrt{\left(N_1\frac{\sqrt{DD}}{RR}\right)^2 + \left(N_2\frac{\sqrt{DR}}{RR}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{N_1DD-N_2DR}{RR^{1.5}}\right)^2} \]

where

\[ N_1 = \frac{f_Rn_R(f_Rn_R-1)}{n_D(n_D-1)} \]

and

\[ N_2 = \frac{f_Rn_R(f_Rn_R-1)}{n_Rn_D} \]

DD, RR and DR are the un-normalised (weighted) numbers of data-data, random-random and data-random pairs, respectively. \(n_D\) and \(n_R\) are the total (weighted) number of data and random objects, respectively. \(f_R=n_{R,dil}/n_R\) is the fraction between the total (weighted) number of random objects and the diluted one (used to improve performances when counting the random-random pairs).

the Poisson error

This function currently works with only the natural and Landy&Szalay estimators
